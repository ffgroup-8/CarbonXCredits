  • CTA: Latest Projects → /carbon-x-credits-projects

📌 Module 3 — Transforming Liability into Opportunity (3 cards)

  • Card 1 (Methane Leaks Are an Untapped Climate Risk): Over 2 million inactive oil and gas wells exist across the U.S., leaking more than 20 million metric tons of CO2e methane annually (EPA). Methane is 84x more impactful than CO2 over 20 years (IPCC AR6). Carbon X identifies, quantifies, and neutralizes this risk, converting unmanaged methane leaks into certified carbon credits.
  • Card 2 (No Market Signal, No Action, Until Now): The clean energy transition is accelerating, driven by AI workloads, electrification, and ESG accountability. Legacy oilfield emissions remain a blind spot with no compliance mandates. Carbon X aligns emissions reduction with monetizable carbon assets, delivering climate action at the speed and scale this new energy economy requires.
  • Card 3 (A National Infrastructure Opportunity): As of 2025, over 500,000 wells have sat inactive for five years or more. In 2020, abandoned wells released roughly 24 million metric tons of CO2e, equivalent to 5.3 million cars. Carbon X's vertically integrated model turns this fragmented infrastructure into permanent methane abatement and credit generation.

📌 Module 4 — Mission Deep-Dive

  • Eyebrow: Our Mission
  • Headline: Carbon markets are an effective financial mechanism to scale environmental remediation
  • Body: Carbon X targets high-risk, methane-leaking wells using historical production data, site conditions, and precision detection. Wells are permanently sealed to eliminate emissions, with avoided methane quantified using certified methodologies and verified equipment. Many are non-core assets: low-producing, aging infrastructure acquired out of bankruptcy by small operators with limited capital. Rather than waiting on policy, Carbon X converts unmanaged methane risks into monetizable carbon assets that fund cleanup today.
  • CTA: Core Team → /about

📌 Module 5 — Solutions (4-step process)

  • Eyebrow / Headline: Solutions · How we are solving this
  • Intro: We identify and plug high-emission wells across the U.S. and turn those results into verified carbon credits that fund further cleanup.
  • 1. Identify: Wells are selected through a proprietary screening process evaluating gas production history, current status, site accessibility, and emissions potential, filtered by economic models for minimum credit thresholds.
  • 2. Measure: Methane is measured using EPA Method 21 standards with certified TDLAS equipment, capturing pre- and post-plugging readings with GPS and timestamp verification, internally reviewed and third-party validated.
  • 3. Plug: Wells are sealed with multi-barrier isolation strategies (mechanical and cement-based plugs) that exceed state standards, overseen by regulatory agencies and validated through tagging, testing, and third-party review.
  • 4. Monetize: Verified emissions data is converted into carbon credits through leading global registries. Credit proceeds are reinvested into future plugging projects, creating a sustainable engine for climate remediation.

📌 Module 6 — Benefits of our process (3 cards)

  • Methane Abatement at Scale: Carbon X permanently seals high-emitting wells across the U.S., targeting the most impactful sites first with advanced detection and industry-leading plugging standards.
  • Verified Carbon Credits: Every credit is backed by measurable, trackable, permanent methane reduction, using certified methodologies, precision instrumentation, and multiple layers of third-party verification.
  • Full Lifecycle Execution: From leak detection and baseline measurement to plugging, credit development, and market delivery, the entire process is managed in-house under a vertically integrated model.

📌 Module 7 — How Carbon X Compares

  • Subhead: Our vertically integrated approach ensures maximum efficiency, impact, and quality, delivering measurable and verifiable credits.
  • Rows: Project Control (turnkey execution vs project development only) · Emissions Monitoring (measured before and after every plug vs varies) · Verification Standards (internal, registry-based, and third-party vs generic or minimum) · Permanence Assurance (cement tags, long-term emissions profiling, integrated oversight vs not provided).
